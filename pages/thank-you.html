Before we walk the line

Five quick things to think about.

None of this is required — but the more clarity you bring to the walkthrough, the tighter the quote, the faster the build, and the less back-and-forth later.

01The why

What's the fence solving?

Privacy from a new build next door. A dog that's already escaped twice. Pool inspection in three weeks. A house going on the market in spring.

Why it matters: The "why" tells us the right material, height, and code requirements before we even pull a tape measure.
02The line

Know roughly where it goes.

Survey stakes still in the ground? Property lines marked? Old fence to follow? If you have a plat or survey PDF on your closing docs, dig it up — text it to (757) 597-4513 ahead of time.

Why it matters: Saves 30 minutes of detective work the day we walk it. We'll still verify on-site — but we'll be fast about it.
03The HOA / utilities

Any rules in play?

HOA covenants on style, height, or color? Underground utilities you already know about (sprinklers, gas line to the grill, low-voltage)? Easements?

Heads up: HOA paperwork is yours to submit — your board will want it from the homeowner directly. We'll provide spec sheets, drawings, and color samples for your packet. We always call 811 for utility locates before we dig.

04The look

Bring a photo or two.

A neighbor's fence you love. A Pinterest board. An Instagram screenshot. "I want it to look like that, but without the lattice top." Vague is fine — visual is better than verbal.

Why it matters: Saves you from picking from a brochure of options that don't include the one you actually want.
05The timeline

When do you need it done?

Closing date. Pool inspection. Puppy arriving. Wedding in the backyard. Just "this summer." Hard deadline or flexible?

Why it matters: Material lead times vary (cedar = 3-5 days, ornamental aluminum = 2-3 weeks). Knowing your deadline up front means we can spec around it instead of rushing materials.
06The decision-makers

Who else has a say?

Spouse, partner, neighbor on a shared line, business partner, board approval? If anyone else needs to weigh in, the walkthrough is the easiest time to get them in the loop.

Why it matters: Saves a second visit. Same-day quote means same-day decision — if everyone who needs to see it has seen it.
